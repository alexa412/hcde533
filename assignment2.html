Assignment 2: Parametric Press Fit Construction Kit!

by Alexa Schlein for HCDE 533, October 2022

Finished Kit


Process

Like most people in our class, I'm not really used to parametric modeling - certainly not in Grasshopper. So, I decided to create a flower-like shape in Grasshopper that I thought would assemble well.

Grasshopper Parametric Model

I created my shape in Grasshopper with a polar array of circles and a polar array of rectangles (~0.4 in depth). Step 1: I used the polar array of circles, a pentagon component, and a trim with regions component to make the general floral shape.
Step 2: Then, I used a polar array of rectangles and and a vtrim with regions component to make the slots.

Step 3: Then, I joined the curves and used an extrude component to extrude the shape.

The cardboard thickness is set by a number slider component. Using a subtract component, I subtract the cardboard thickness by 0.020 in for the slot width for a press fit. So, if you change the cardboard thickness, the slot width will adjust as well. See my definitions below!

Laser Cutting

Prototyping

To figure out the right dimensions for the extrusion, I took several measurements and found the average of the cardboard thickness: 0.125 +/- 0.009 in. I set the cardboard thickness in Grasshopper, and then baked the into Grasshopper. Then, I exported the shape into an Adobe Illustrator file.

Laser Cutting

I first ran a test cut, cutting 2 components, to make sure the dimensions and press fit were appropriate. I cut out my components using the laser cutter in the 8. I used the settings for mat board, but I set the material thickness to 0.45 in for cutting. My parts didn't catch on fire! I had to cut on 2 pieces of cardboard because my cardboard pieces were taken from a smaller Amazon box.

Reflection

I didn't need to make my slots as long as I did. Also, I didn't include a chamfer or fillet on my edges because I couldn't figure out how to do it in Grasshopper. The insertion was a bit difficult and a fillet would've helped. Maybe next time! I wanted to know if I could use a number slider to change the number of circles for the polar array component as well, but I ran into trouble with the polygon component that's used for trimming.
