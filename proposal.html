Final Project Proposal

by Alexa Schlein for HCDE 533, November 2022

Bees, Flowers, and Honeycomb Jewelry Set

Idea and Inspiration

I'm feeling a little bit guilty about the plastic waste from my acrylic laser cut lamp project, so I wanted to create something that combines leftover materials with a related theme. Two of the shapes I created for the lamp shade are flowers. So, I came up with a bee and flower-inspired jewelry set. I saw plenty of honeycomb-style pendants and earrings on Etsy, so I'm going to incorporate a honeycomb pattern designed in Grasshopper for my pieces.

Image Source


Honeycomb Ring

The 3D printed ring will either be honeycomb the entire way around, or just the part that sits on top of my finger. The ring will be size 7.5 (17.7mm) to 8 (18.2mm) to fit me.

Honeycomb Earrings

The earrings will be flat, 3D printed honeycomb shapes that are attached to earring hooks. I may glue the 3D print to a laser cut piece from my leftover fluorescent acrylic (stretch goal).

Bee + Flower Necklace

I'll be using flowers leftover from my lamp project, and 3D printing a (likely) flat bee shape. I'll chain/glue together all pieces, and then attach them to a necklace chain.

Context for Use

I'm designing the jewelry to be worn by me! Although, anyone with a similar ring size or measurements could wear the jewelry!

Stretch Goal: Jewelry Dish

Idea and Inspiration

In the same vein as my jewelry idea, I do want to reuse more of my acrylic pieces by building a laser cut and 3D printed jewelry dish. This is a stretch goal for me in case I don't have time to make it to the MILL. The base of the dish is 3 layers of acrylic; the middle layer is filled with my leftover floral pieces. The layers will be held together with a Chicago screw (and glue, if necessary). The laser cut base will sit inside a 3D printed outer portion.

Context for Use

The dish will sit alongside my already sprawling jewelry collection.
